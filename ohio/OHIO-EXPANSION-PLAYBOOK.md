# Ohio Expansion Playbook — OverAssessed LLC

## Market Overview
- **88 counties**, all use same DTE Form 1
- **Filing window:** October 30 → March 31 (5 full months to market)
- **Deadline:** March 31 annually (Ohio Revised Code 5715.19)
- **Population:** 11.8M (7th largest state)
- **Top markets:** Columbus (Franklin), Cleveland (Cuyahoga), Cincinnati (Hamilton), Dayton (Montgomery), Toledo (Lucas), Akron (Summit)

## Pricing
- **Residential:** 30% of first-year savings
- **Commercial:** 35% of first-year savings
- **No savings = no charge**
- Competitive vs Ownwell (25-35%) and tax attorneys ($250-500 + 25%)

## Process (Per Case)
1. Client submits property at overassessed.ai/ohio
2. We pull assessed value + comps from county auditor
3. Generate evidence packet with comparable properties
4. Pre-fill DTE Form 1
5. E-file as agent (Line 3) OR email to county BOR
6. If hearing scheduled → prep client for remote video hearing
7. Client presents (we can't testify on value as paid agent per Rule 10)
8. Decision in writing → calculate savings → invoice 30%

## Legal/Licensing
- **No license required** to file as agent in Ohio
- DTE Form 1 Line 3 = "Complainant's Agent" (explicitly supported)
- Agent signs under penalty of perjury
- Franklin County: **no notarization required** (waived)
- Other counties: may still require notarization (check per county)

## Hearing Rules (Franklin County — may vary by county)
- **Default: 100% remote** via live video streaming
- In-person available only if requested 10+ days ahead
- Agent CAN attend but **cannot testify on value** if compensated (Rule 10)
- Owner or Ohio-licensed attorney should present
- Evidence must be submitted 7 business days before hearing
- All hearings are video recorded and public
- Decision appealable to BTA or Court of Common Pleas within 30 days

## Reappraisal Schedule (Key Insight)
Ohio does triennial reappraisals. Counties on different cycles:
- **2023 reappraisal counties** (values just jumped): Franklin, Cuyahoga, Hamilton + others
- These are the BEST targets — homeowners saw big increases and are most motivated
- Next reappraisal for these counties: 2026
- **Strategy:** Target counties in the year AFTER their reappraisal when values spike
