## Post 1: PSA About May 15 Protest Deadline

**Category:** General | Real Estate

Hey neighbors 👋

Quick PSA — if you got your property tax assessment notice from BCAD recently, you only have until **May 15** to file a protest. After that, you're stuck paying whatever they say your home is worth for the whole year.

I know it's easy to toss that letter in the junk pile, but it's worth 5 minutes to look at the number. If your assessed value jumped more than ~5-8%, you probably have grounds to protest.

You can file online at the Bexar County Appraisal District website. It's free to file — worst case they say no and you pay the same amount you would have anyway.

Don't leave money on the table! Happy to answer questions if anyone has them.

## Post 4: How-To for First-Timers

**Category:** General | Real Estate

For anyone who's never protested their property taxes before — here's the quick version since I get asked about this every year:

**Step 1:** Check your assessment notice from BCAD (should have arrived by mail or you can look it up online at bcad.org)

**Step 2:** Compare your assessed value to actual recent sales of similar homes nearby. Zillow or Redfin work fine for a quick gut check.

**Step 3:** If your value looks high, file a protest before May 15. You can do it online through BCAD's website — takes about 10 minutes.

**Step 4:** Gather your evidence. Photos of any issues with your home, comparable sales data, anything that shows your value should be lower.

**Step 5:** Attend your informal hearing (or have someone represent you). Most protests get resolved here without going to a formal hearing.

**Pro tips:**
- You do NOT need a reason to protest. "Value is too high" is enough.
- Even if you're not sure, file anyway. You literally cannot go up by protesting.
- If the process feels overwhelming, there are services that handle the whole thing for a percentage of savings. I used overassessed.ai and it was painless.

Good luck! 🏠
