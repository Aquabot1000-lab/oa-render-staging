# Google Ads Campaign Structure — Georgia Property Tax Protest

## Campaign Overview

| Setting | Value |
|---------|-------|
| **Campaign Name** | OA - GA Property Tax Protest - Search |
| **Campaign Type** | Search |
| **Networks** | Google Search only (no Display, no Search Partners) |
| **Locations** | Georgia, United States |
| **Bid Strategy** | Maximize Conversions (switch to Target CPA after 30+ conversions) |
| **Daily Budget** | $75–$100/day ($2,250–$3,000/mo) — scale after CPA stabilizes |

## Budget Recommendation

### Phase 1: Launch (Weeks 1–4)
- **Daily Budget:** $75/day ($2,250/mo)
- **Bid Strategy:** Maximize Conversions
- **Goal:** Collect conversion data, identify winning ad groups
- **Focus:** General + Fulton County + Value Freeze ad groups

### Phase 2: Optimize (Weeks 5–8)
- **Daily Budget:** $100/day ($3,000/mo)
- **Bid Strategy:** Switch to Target CPA once you hit 30+ conversions
- **Goal:** Reduce CPA, scale winning ad groups
- **Action:** Pause low-performing keywords, add negative keywords from search terms report

### Phase 3: Scale (Weeks 9+)
- **Daily Budget:** $150–$200/day ($4,500–$6,000/mo)
- **Bid Strategy:** Target CPA
- **Goal:** Maximize volume at profitable CPA
- **Action:** Launch remaining county ad groups, test new ad copy

### Key Metrics to Track
| Metric | Target |
|--------|--------|
| CPC | $3–$8 |
| CTR | >4% |
| Conversion Rate | >5% |
| Cost per Lead | <$50 |
| Target CPA | $30–$45 |

### Notes
- GA property tax appeal season runs roughly April–July (assessment notices go out in spring)
- Increase budget 30% during peak season (April–June)
- Monitor Fulton County closely — highest search volume in GA
- The 299(c) value freeze angle is unique — expect strong CTR on that ad group
