Frequently Asked Questions

Everything Washington homeowners need to know

📊 How are Washington properties assessed?

Washington state assesses property at 100% of fair market value. Your county assessor determines this value annually based on comparable sales, market trends, and property characteristics. If the assessor overestimates your home's value, you're overpaying in taxes.

⏰ When is the appeal deadline?

Appeal deadlines vary by county but are typically 30 to 60 days after you receive your valuation notice (usually mailed in late spring or early summer). In King County, you have 60 days. In Pierce and Snohomish, it's typically 30 days. Sign up early — don't wait until the deadline.

💰 How is your fee calculated?

We charge 20% of your actual property tax savings. If we reduce your assessed value and you save $1,500, our fee is $300. If we don't save you money, you pay nothing. No upfront fees, no admin fees, no hidden costs.

⚠️ Can my value go up if I appeal?

In Washington, the Board of Equalization reviews your assessed value and could potentially adjust it upward. However, this is rare when a well-documented case is presented. We thoroughly analyze every property before filing and will not pursue an appeal if we believe there's risk of an increase.

🏛️ What is the Board of Equalization?

Each Washington county has a Board of Equalization (BOE) that hears property tax appeals. The BOE is an independent body that reviews your assessed value and can order a reduction if they agree the assessor overvalued your property. We present your case with comparable sales data and market analysis.

📋 Do I need to attend a hearing?

No. We handle the entire process on your behalf, including preparing evidence, filing paperwork, and representing you at hearings if needed. Some counties like King County offer electronic filing through the eAppeals system, making the process even smoother.
