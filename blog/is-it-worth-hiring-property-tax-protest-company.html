Is It Worth Hiring a Property Tax Protest Company?

Published March 16, 2026 · 10 min read · By OverAssessed Team

You just received your property tax assessment notice, and the number is higher than expected. You know you can protest, but you're weighing your options: should you handle the protest yourself, or hire a property tax protest company to do it for you?

It's a fair question — and one that thousands of homeowners across Texas and beyond ask every year. In this article, we'll give you an honest breakdown of the costs, benefits, and trade-offs so you can make the right decision for your situation.

How Property Tax Protest Companies Work

Before diving into whether it's worth it, let's clarify what these companies actually do:

  1. You authorize them to act on your behalf — Typically through a simple online sign-up and authorization form
  2. They research your property — Pulling comparable sales, equity data, and checking for errors in your appraisal district records
  3. They file your protest — Before the deadline, with the appropriate grounds selected
  4. They prepare your evidence — Building a professional evidence packet with comps, photos, and market analysis
  5. They attend hearings on your behalf — At both informal and formal (ARB) hearings, so you don't have to take time off work
  6. They pursue further appeals if needed — Some companies will escalate to binding arbitration or district court

The key feature of most reputable companies is their contingency-based pricing: you only pay if they successfully reduce your taxes. No reduction = no fee.

The Pros of Hiring a Property Tax Protest Company

1. Zero Risk, Guaranteed

With contingency-based pricing, you literally cannot lose money. If the company fails to lower your taxes, you pay nothing. This makes the decision essentially risk-free from a financial perspective.

2. Time Savings

A thorough DIY protest requires:

  • 2–4 hours researching comparable sales and building your evidence
  • 1–2 hours filing the protest and understanding the process
  • 2–4 hours attending the informal and/or formal hearing (including travel and wait time)
  • Additional time if you need to appeal the ARB decision

That's 5–10+ hours of your time. If you value your time at $50/hour or more, a $240 fee to save you 8 hours is a good deal.

3. Professional Expertise and Better Data

Property tax protest companies do this every day. They have:

  • Access to MLS data and professional comp databases that aren't available to the public
  • Experience knowing which arguments work best with specific appraisal districts
  • Relationships with appraisers and understanding of local hearing dynamics
  • Knowledge of technical strategies (equity arguments, cost approaches) that homeowners might not know

4. Potentially Larger Reductions

Studies consistently show that professional protests tend to achieve larger reductions than DIY protests. This makes sense — they have better data, more experience, and know how to frame arguments effectively. A professional might get you a $40,000 reduction where a DIY effort might have achieved $25,000.

5. You Don't Have to Miss Work

Hearings happen during business hours. If taking a half-day off work costs you more than the protest fee, hiring a company is the clear financial choice.

6. They Handle Everything

From filing to evidence preparation to hearing attendance to follow-up — you sign up and they take care of the rest. For busy homeowners, this convenience is worth a lot.

The Cons of Hiring a Property Tax Protest Company

1. You Pay a Percentage of Savings

This is the obvious trade-off. If you could have achieved the same reduction yourself, you'd keep 100% of the savings instead of 65–80%. For someone with the time and skill to run a strong protest, this is money left on the table.

2. Quality Varies Widely

Not all property tax protest companies are created equal. Some common issues:

  • Volume-based operations that batch-process thousands of cases with minimal individual attention
  • Automated systems that generate cookie-cutter evidence without human analysis
  • Acceptance of low-ball offers at informal hearings to close cases quickly
  • Poor communication — some companies don't update you on your case status

3. You Lose Some Control

When you authorize a company to handle your protest, they make decisions on your behalf. Some homeowners prefer to be directly involved in negotiations and hearings.

4. Some Companies Have Minimum Fees or Hidden Costs

While most reputable companies operate on pure contingency, some charge minimum fees ($50–$100) regardless of the outcome, or have hidden administrative fees. Always read the fine print.

When Hiring a Company Is Clearly Worth It

Based on the analysis above, hiring a property tax protest company is worth it when:

  1. You don't have time to research, file, and attend hearings yourself
  2. Your property is high-value (above $300,000) — the potential savings are large enough that the fee is a small fraction of your benefit
  3. You've never protested before and don't know the process
  4. You're not comfortable presenting at a hearing or negotiating with an appraiser
  5. Missing work for a hearing would cost more than the protest fee
  6. You want to maximize your reduction rather than just get "something"
  7. The company charges a low percentage (20%) on a no-win, no-fee basis

When DIY Makes More Sense

Protesting yourself makes more sense when:

  1. You have time and enjoy the process — Some homeowners genuinely enjoy the research and negotiation
  2. Your case is straightforward — An obvious data error (wrong square footage) doesn't require professional help
  3. Your property value is low — On a $150,000 home, the potential savings and resulting fee may be small enough that DIY is the better value
  4. You have strong evidence already — A recent professional appraisal showing a lower value is hard to argue against, with or without a company
  5. You've protested successfully before and know the process well

What to Look for When Choosing a Company

If you decide to hire a protest company, here are the key factors to evaluate:

Fee Structure

  • Contingency-based only — No upfront fees, no payment if no savings
  • Low percentage — 20% is the industry best; avoid companies charging 30%+ when better options exist
  • No hidden fees — Watch for "administrative fees," "filing fees," or minimum charges

Service Quality

  • Personal attention vs. fully automated — Does a human actually review your case?
  • Evidence quality — Do they use professional comp databases or just Zillow?
  • Hearing attendance — Do they attend both informal and formal hearings?
  • Communication — Will they update you on your case progress?

Reputation

  • Check Google reviews, BBB ratings, and social media feedback
  • Look for transparency about their process and results
  • Ask about their success rate and average savings

Coverage

  • Do they serve your specific county and state?
  • Do they have experience with your local appraisal district?

The Bottom Line

For the vast majority of homeowners, hiring a property tax protest company is absolutely worth it — especially one that charges a low contingency fee with no upfront cost. The math is simple:

  • Zero risk: You only pay if they save you money
  • Time savings: 5–10+ hours of your life back
  • Better results: Professionals typically achieve larger reductions
  • Ongoing savings: The fee is one-time, but the reduction recurs for years

The only scenario where DIY clearly wins is if you have the time, knowledge, and strong evidence already in hand — and even then, the difference in cost is often marginal compared to the convenience.

The real question isn't whether to hire a protest company. It's whether to protest at all. And the answer to that is almost always yes.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I have to pay a property tax protest company upfront?

Not with reputable companies. OverAssessed and similar services work on contingency — you pay nothing unless they successfully reduce your taxes. Be wary of any company that asks for money before results.

What percentage do property tax protest companies charge?

Fees typically range from 20% to 35% of your first-year tax savings. OverAssessed charges 20%, which is the lowest among major providers. See our comparison with Ownwell for detailed fee differences.

Can a protest company guarantee a reduction?

No ethical company guarantees a specific outcome. However, the majority of protests result in some reduction. The contingency model means the company is incentivized to work hard on your case — they don't get paid unless they deliver results.

Is it worth protesting a small increase?

Usually, yes. Even a $10,000 reduction in appraised value can save $200–$300 per year in Texas. Over 5 years, that's $1,000–$1,500 — well worth the effort, especially with a no-risk professional handling the work.

Can I switch protest companies if I'm not happy?

Yes, in most cases. Review your authorization agreement for any termination clauses, but you generally have the right to revoke authorization and either protest yourself or switch to a different company.
